Interpretation of Results

      The result of the model is found using the accuracy and loss generated by our model for both the training and validation sets and comparing them to each other. The loss of a model is the sum of the differences between the actual values and the predicted values, whereas the accuracy tells us how many of the data points we correctly classified. This helps interpretability in the sense of showing us what the problem in our model is in four ways

  • High loss, low accuracy — we are making big errors on large proportion of data
  • High loss, high accuracy — we are making big errors on a small proportion of data
  • Low loss, low accuracy — we are making small errors on a large proportion of data
  • Low loss, high accuracy — we are making small errors on small proportion of data

      There have been many studies and experiments done on sea animal classification using Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs). Overall, CNNs have proven to be highly effective in accurately identifying and classifying different species of sea animals based on their images.One study published in the Journal of Marine Science and Engineering used a CNN to classify six different species of sea animals: dolphin, fish, jellyfish, octopus, sea turtle, and shark. The researchers used a dataset of 5,000 images, which was split into training and testing sets. They achieved an accuracy of 95.9% on the testing set, which is a highly accurate result.

      Another study published in the Journal of Marine Science and Engineering used a CNN to classify five different species of sea turtles: green turtle, hawksbill turtle, leatherback turtle, loggerhead turtle, and olive ridley turtle. The researchers used a dataset of 600 images, which was also split into training and testing sets. They achieved an accuracy of 94.5% on the testing set, which is also highly accurate.
